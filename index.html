Faces in the Food

J115 Dev Assignment

by Chase McCleary

The Unphased Eggplant
This is a picture of a slightly confused, yet seemingly content eggplant. The oblong curve on the front resembles a nose, the indentations resemble two eyes and a Mona-Lisa mouth, and the stem looks like a bit of scruffed green hair. Though this is clearly a terrific plant, there are a few possibilities that might explain what caused this mishappenness, including low moisture and/or high temperature conditions. For maximized production, it is suggested that one remove the the fruit before they are fully mature, giving the plant more room to develop additional fruit.
The Overconfident Raddish
This is a picture of an overly confident raddish. I dislike his posture against that fine piece of china. The raddish deserves to be cooked and eaten, though I am sure its taste would likely be described as bitter and unpleasant. However, these oddly-shappen vegetables have become something of an icon in Japan. Dubbed "nigeru daikon", or "Running Away Raddish," these legged roots have taken Japanese social media by storm. They have inspired an influx of nigeru daikon photos where users pose them in various running positions.
